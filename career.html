Career Support

Introduction to Career Development

Career development is an ongoing process that involves exploring interests, building professional skills, and preparing for opportunities after graduation. The University of Michigan School of Information (UMSI) provides a wide range of services designed to help students discover potential career paths and develop the tools needed to succeed in a competitive job market.

Through advising, workshops, networking opportunities, and employer partnerships, students gain the confidence and practical experience needed to transition from academic study to professional careers. These resources help students reflect on their strengths, identify career goals, and connect with professionals working across a variety of industries.

Career Resources

Below are several key resources provided by the University of Michigan School of Information. These services support students through one-on-one coaching, skill-building workshops, and data about career outcomes for graduates.

  • Career Coaching
    UMSI career coaches work directly with students to develop personalized career strategies. Students can schedule appointments to discuss resumes, cover letters, internship searches, interview preparation, and long-term career planning. Coaching sessions provide individualized feedback and help students build confidence when navigating the job market.
  • Career Education
    Career education opportunities include workshops, online resources, and structured learning sessions focused on professional development. Topics include self-assessment, career exploration, salary negotiation, professional communication, and building a strong online presence through platforms like LinkedIn.

  • Career Outcomes
    UMSI graduates pursue careers across a wide range of sectors including technology companies, government agencies, nonprofit organizations, and consulting firms. Career outcome reports provide valuable insight into employment rates, average salaries, and the types of roles students secure after completing their degrees.

Exploring Career Paths

Students in information and data-related fields can pursue many different career paths depending on their interests and skills. Some common roles include data analyst, UX researcher, product manager, information architect, digital archivist, and policy analyst. Career exploration allows students to understand how their academic training can translate into real-world professional opportunities.

Networking events, employer information sessions, and alumni panels help students learn directly from professionals working in the field. These experiences provide valuable insight into workplace expectations and emerging trends in information and technology careers.

Building Professional Skills

In addition to academic coursework, developing professional skills is essential for long-term career success. UMSI encourages students to gain practical experience through internships, research opportunities, and collaborative projects. These experiences allow students to apply classroom knowledge to real-world problems while strengthening their resumes.

Key professional skills include communication, teamwork, problem solving, and technical proficiency in tools such as data analysis software, programming languages, and design platforms. Developing these skills prepares students to adapt to rapidly changing industries and evolving professional environments.
